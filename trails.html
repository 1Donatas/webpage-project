Popular Hiking Trails in the Peak District

High Peak Trail

This 17.5-mile trail follows the route of the former Cromford and High Peak Railway, offering a journey through the heart of the Peak District's industrial heritage. Suitable for walkers, cyclists, and horse riders, the trail provides expansive views of the surrounding countryside.

Highlights: Steep inclines, historic railway structures, and panoramic vistas.

Difficulty: Moderate; some sections have steep gradients.

Starting Point: Dowlow, south of Buxton.

Monsal Trail

Stretching 8.5 miles along a former railway line, the Monsal Trail features several tunnels, viaducts, and bridges. It's a flat, accessible route ideal for families, offering stunning views of the Wye Valley.

Highlights: Lit tunnels, Monsal Viaduct, and limestone dales.

Difficulty: Easy; mostly flat and well-surfaced.

Starting Point: Coombs Road, Bakewell.

Tissington Trail

Following the route of the former Buxton to Ashbourne railway, this 13-mile trail passes through the picturesque village of Tissington. It's popular among walkers, cyclists, and horse riders, offering gentle gradients and scenic countryside views.

Highlights: Historic stations, wildflower meadows, and the charming Tissington village.

Difficulty: Easy; suitable for all abilities.

Starting Point: Mapleton Lane, Ashbourne.

Kinder Scout via Edale

This challenging 8-mile circular route ascends to the highest point in the Peak District, Kinder Scout. The trail offers rugged terrain, peat bogs, and sweeping moorland views. It's a favorite among experienced hikers seeking a demanding trek.

Highlights: Grindsbrook Clough, Kinder Downfall, and panoramic vistas.

Difficulty: Hard; involves steep climbs and navigation skills.

Starting Point: Edale Village.

Dovedale

One of the most famous beauty spots in the Peak District, Dovedale offers a 3-mile picturesque walk along the River Dove, passing through limestone ravines and over its famous stepping stones.

Highlights: Stepping stones, limestone formations, and scenic river views.

Difficulty: Easy; suitable for families.

Starting Point: Dovedale Car Park.

Stanage Edge

A favorite among rock climbers and hikers, Stanage Edge is a dramatic gritstone escarpment offering breathtaking views over the Dark Peak area. The route is around 5 miles and follows the ridge, making it perfect for scenic photography.

Highlights: Stunning rock formations, expansive views, and historic millstones.

Difficulty: Moderate; some rocky sections.

Starting Point: Hathersage.

Mam Tor and the Great Ridge

This 5-mile ridge walk is one of the most famous in the Peak District, offering breathtaking views over the Hope Valley and Edale.

Highlights: Stunning ridge walk, sunrise views, and Iron Age hill fort.

Difficulty: Moderate; steady ascent but well-paved paths.

Starting Point: Mam Nick Car Park.

Winnats Pass and Cave Dale

A 4.5-mile circular trail featuring the dramatic limestone gorge of Winnats Pass and the atmospheric Cave Dale.

Highlights: Limestone valley, caves, and stunning rock formations.

Difficulty: Moderate; some rocky and uneven terrain.

Starting Point: Castleton.

Ladybower Reservoir Circular

A scenic 5-mile walk around Ladybower Reservoir, offering beautiful waterside views and rolling countryside.

Highlights: Dam views, woodlands, and reflections on the water.

Difficulty: Easy; flat and well-maintained paths.

Starting Point: Fairholmes Visitor Centre.

Chrome Hill and Parkhouse Hill

Known as the "Dragon's Back," this 5-mile hike features steep limestone ridges and breathtaking scenery.

Highlights: Sharp ridges, rolling hills, and iconic Peak District views.

Difficulty: Hard; steep ascents and rugged terrain.

Starting Point: Hollinsclough.

Baslow Edge and Curbar Edge

A stunning 6-mile walk along gritstone edges, perfect for spotting red deer and capturing spectacular views.

Highlights: Rock formations, wildlife spotting, and panoramic landscapes.

Difficulty: Moderate; some uneven rocky paths.

Starting Point: Curbar Gap Car Park.

Alport Castles

A 7-mile walk leading to the largest landslip in the UK, featuring dramatic rock formations and rugged landscapes.

Highlights: Towering rock formations and stunning valley views.

Difficulty: Hard; requires endurance and navigation.

Starting Point: Fairholmes Car Park.

Thor’s Cave and Manifold Valley

A fascinating 4-mile trail leading to the iconic Thor’s Cave, with beautiful valley views along the way.

Highlights: Enormous cave entrance, valley views, and rock formations.

Difficulty: Moderate; steep climb to the cave.

Starting Point: Wetton Car Park.

The Roaches and Lud’s Church

A 7-mile adventure through the rocky escarpment of The Roaches and the mysterious moss-covered chasm of Lud’s Church.

Highlights: Spectacular rock formations, panoramic views, and a hidden gorge.

Difficulty: Moderate; some rocky terrain and climbing.

Starting Point: Roaches Gate.
